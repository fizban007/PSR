Dipole and Quadrupole Magnetic Fields for Pulsars

This is an interactive tool to help build intuition on how different components of dipole and quadrupole magnetic fields superimpose near the surface of a pulsar, and how the polar caps change as a result.

All field lines are color-coded. Green lines are closed field lines and light blue one are open. We define open field lines as those reaching the light cylinder and beyond. One can adjust the size of the light cylinder by changing the parameter LC. The star always has radius 1. Not all field lines are shown. This is to improve performance of both rendering and integration. To show more field lines, reduce the min_length parameter.

Here are the field lines of an inclined dipole. By default the dipole is inclined in the $yz$ plane. The default view is from the $x$ axis.

Here are the field lines of a pure quadrupole. We adopt the Cartesian quadrupole tensor defined as:

\[ \mathbf{Q} = \begin{pmatrix} q_{11} & q_{12} & q_{13} \\ q_{12} & q_{22} & q_{23} \\ q_{13} & q_{23} & -q_{11} - q_{22} \end{pmatrix} \]

The quadrupole magnetic field is then defined using a static magnetic potential:

\[ \mathbf{B}_q = -\nabla\left(\frac{\mathbf{r}\mathbf{Q}\mathbf{r}^T}{r^5}\right) = -\frac{2}{r^5}\mathbf{Q}\mathbf{r} + \frac{5}{r^7}(\mathbf{r}\mathbf{Q}\mathbf{r}^T)\mathbf{r} \]
